Old Port Of Montreal

Where history meets fun by the St.Lawrence River

History

The port dates back to the early 1600s, when French settlers used it as a key hub for trade and exploratoin. It was Montreal's main commercial harbor for centuries until it was rdeveloped into a cultural and recreational space.

Atmosphere

Cobbleston streets, historic warehouses turned into boutiques, museums, cafes, and waterfront paths give it a mix of historic charm and modern leisure.

Stroll the waterfront

Beautiful walking and cycling paths with views of the river and skyline.

La Grande Roue de Monrtreal

A giant observation wheel with amazing views of the city and river

Boat cruises

Sightseeing tours, dinner cruises, and river shuttles operate from the port.

Outdoor activities

In summer, kayaking, pedal boats, and zip-lining; in winter, ice skating rinks and festive lights.

Cultural sites

The Montreal Science Center and the IMAX Theatre are located there, great for families.

Food and cafe

From casual snacks to find dinning, often with terraces overlooking the water.
